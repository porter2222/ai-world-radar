# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AI World Radar serves Chinese AI learners, early Agent / LLM application developers, product newcomers, students, independent developers, and AI enthusiasts who want to understand what is happening in the global AI scene without constantly following English-language sources such as Hacker News, X, GitHub, YouTube, newsletters, or overseas media.

These users are usually exploring, learning, and trying to keep up. They need fast orientation, restrained explanation, and reliable source trails more than exhaustive coverage or entertainment.

## Product Purpose

AI World Radar is a global AI event intelligence radar for Chinese users.

The product helps users understand the most important recent AI events in about five minutes: what happened, why it matters, where the information came from, what remains uncertain, and what may need continued observation.

It is allowed to borrow the browsing efficiency of an information aggregation product, but it must go beyond ordinary aggregation. Its differentiator is the event dossier: source signals are transformed through an Agent workflow into readable, traceable, and reviewable AI event intelligence.

Success means the interface feels trustworthy enough to read daily, clear enough to scan quickly, and engineered enough to show the Agent production chain behind the published content.

## Brand Personality

Clear-headed, trustworthy, restrained.

The product should feel like a calm AI intelligence desk: professional, current, structured, and useful. It should not shout, oversell, or decorate for its own sake. It should still feel alive and modern, but its confidence comes from hierarchy, evidence, and workflow clarity rather than visual noise.

## Anti-references

AI World Radar should not become a generic AI news scraper that only republishes links and summaries.

It should not look like an AI tool directory, buying guide, affiliate portal, or commercial content hub.

It should not copy the structure of reference sites as a layout skin. Reference screenshots may inform cleanliness and density, but the product must establish its own event-intelligence structure.

It should not use dark cyberpunk styling, purple-blue AI gradients, glassmorphism, generic SaaS hero sections, or dashboard decoration that makes the product feel like a demo rather than a credible tool.

It should not make the homepage feel like a pure traffic ranking board. Heat is useful, but the interface should frame heat as one signal inside an event judgment system.

## Design Principles

1. Browsing efficiency, intelligence depth.
   The homepage may scan like an information product, but every path should lead toward event understanding rather than headline consumption.

2. Cards are entry points, not reports.
   Homepage cards should answer what happened, what category it belongs to, and whether it is worth opening. They should not carry the full explanation.

3. Details should feel like event dossiers.
   Detail pages should provide context, discussion focus, uncertainty, timeline or follow-up signals when useful, and source trails. They should not read like ordinary news article pages.

4. Show the production chain when it helps trust.
   Agent runs, source signals, quality gate results, review states, and manual fallback should appear in admin and management surfaces, and lightly surface in public pages only when it improves user confidence.

5. Restraint is the default.
   Color, motion, charts, and visual effects must clarify state or hierarchy. Decorative AI aesthetics are rejected.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the default standard.

Text contrast should meet AA requirements, interactive targets should be easy to use on desktop and mobile, focus states must be visible, and important status should not rely on color alone. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences. Chinese copy must remain readable under realistic text lengths, including longer event titles, source names, and review reasons.
